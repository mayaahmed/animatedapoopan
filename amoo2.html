Chapter 2

Greed for Appams

One day Amooma made appams (rice cakes). She made five of them. After eating two each both Amooma and Apoopan wanted the fifth appam for themselves.

"Let us make a deal. We will both lie down and keep quiet with our eyes shut. The first one to utter a sound or make any kind of movement will not get the appam" said Apoopan.

"Agreed" said Amooma.

So the two of them laid down making no sound, with their eyes shut, and hardly breathing. A neighbor who wanted to borrow some match sticks came and found them thus. All her attempts to get some response from them failed. She panicked and made a big ruckus. The villagers gathered around thinking that Amooma and Apoopan were dead.

"Let us bury them" said the priest.

The villagers got a pair of coffins in a hurry. They put Amooma and Apoopan in the coffins and took them to the burial ground. But when it was time to shut the coffin Apoopan's coffin turned out to be a little small.

"Let us just break his leg" said one man. "After all he is dead."

"Let us do that" said another.

All the villagers agreed to break Apoopan's leg to make him fit in the coffin. Hearing this Apoopan jumped up with a start and said

"No, no, no! Please spare my legs."

"I win the appam" shouted Amooma and ran towards the house.

But when Amooma reached home to eat her appam there was no appam left.

"I thought since you were dead, I would rather eat the appam" said the neighbor's kid. "I must admit it was quite good."

"Greed gets you nothing except trouble" said Amooma.
