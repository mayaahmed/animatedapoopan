Chapter 13

May their souls rest in peace

Amooma's mother died. But before she died she had an urge to eat mangoes. When Amooma told this to the village priest, he said

"Now your mother's soul will find no peace. If you want her soul to find peace, you must make a golden mango and give it to a holy man like me."

"I am a poor woman. I cannot afford to give golden mangoes to holy men" said Amooma.

"Either that or your mother's soul will be tormented" said the priest.

Apoopan was standing nearby and listening to the conversation quietly.

"I will see what I can do" said Amooma pensively.

"Do whatever you can. It is the matter of making a mother's soul happy" said the priest before leaving.

The priest was almost out of the gate with a big grin on his face, when he felt a spank right on his butt. He turned in surprise to see Apoopan with a big laddle ready to give him another big spank.

"What are you doing?" asked the priest in surprise.

"My father's dying wish was to be spanked. I had no heart to spank him. So now I am fulfilling his heart's desire" said Apoopan.

"That is impossible. I do not believe that your father wanted to be spanked" said the priest angrily.

"Anything is possible in Apoopan's family" said Amooma who was also taken by surprise like the priest.

Apoopan went chasing the priest with a laddle for some time.

"Enough, enough! I will say this mantra and your father will find peace" said the priest finally.

"What about Amooma's mother?" asked Apoopan.

"She will also find peace. I had forgotten all about this mantra" said the priest.

The priest then quickly recited a mantra and went his way.
