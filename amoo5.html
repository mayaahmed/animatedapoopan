Chapter 5

Unpaid service

"Amooma, make me some payasam (rice pudding)" said Apoopan. "I feel like eating payasam."

"You will have to go to the forest and collect some firewood" said Amooma. "Only then can I cook any payasam."

Apoopan went to collect firewood in the forest. Suddenly he saw an elephant in front of him and he was very angry.

"How dare you come and disturb my forest?" the elephant roared.

"I just wanted to collect some firewood. Amooma will make some payasam for me if I do" said Apoopan.

"Does she make good payasam?" asked the elephant.

"Yes" said Apoopan. He went on to elaborate fondly how good the Payasam tasted.

"Your description of the payasam makes my mouth water" said the elephant. "Give me some of it and I will help you collect firewood."

"Sure" said Apoopan.

The elephant gathered firewood and carried it for Apoopan all the way out of the forest.

"When can I come for the payasam?" asked the elephant.

"As soon as you see a smoke emitting from the chimney in my house" said Apoopan.

Amooma made the payasam and the couple sat down to eat.

"We must save some payasam for the elephant" said Apoopan.

"We must certainly do that" said Amooma.

Amooma and Apoopan ate and ate and finally there was no more payasam left.

"Oh my goodness! We finished the payasam. Now what shall we do?" asked Amooma.

The elephant had seen the smoke. Apoopan could hear the elephant approaching the house.

"We must hide quickly" said Apoopan. The couple went and hid in the attic.

The elephant came and saw that Apoopan was missing and the payasam pot was empty. The elephant roared in anger and shook the house violently. But Amooma and Apoopan did not make a sound. After all the futile display of anger the elephant decided to lick the remains of the payasam from the pot. The elephant carried the payasam pot away.

"The elephant is taking my pot away" said Amooma.

"Keep quiet" whispered Apoopan. "Your life is more important than a pot. Do not make a sound. All this dust in the attic is making me want to sneeze really bad. I hope this elephant goes away soon."

The elephant on the way out saw the mango tree full of ripe mangoes in Apoopan's yard. The elephant plucked the mangoes and collected it in the pot.

"He will finish all the mangoes" whispered Apoopan in dismay.

The pot slowly filled with the best mangoes from the tree. As the elephant began to leave with the pot full of mangoes, Apoopan could withhold his sneeze no longer. He sneezed loudly.

The poor elephant thought that someone fired a gun. He left the pot and ran away with all his might.

"The elephant helped us make payasam and he also plucked all these mangoes for us" said Apoopan. "The elephant got nothing in return."

"I also did not lose my pot" said Amooma happily. "This surely was not the elephant's day."
