Chapter 14

Think of me more

One day Apoopan met an old friend after a long time. The two of them went to a tea shop and were talking about this and that when Apoopan's friend got a hiccup.

"My wife must be thinking of me. You get hiccups only when someone is thinking of you" said Apoopan's friend.

A couple of minutes later the friend got another hiccup and he said "My wife must be missing me too much."

Later, when Apoopan reached home, Apoopan complained to Amooma

"You never think of me. I never got a single hiccup. My friend got two hiccups when he was with me for a short time. His wife is a more caring wife than you."

And Apoopan went sulking around the house all day saying

"You do not think of me enough, you do not think of me enough."

Finally, Amooma got angry and decided to teach Apoopan a lesson. The next day as Apoopan was going to the farm, Amooma gave him a lunch box as usual but she added a lot of chili powder to the curry. After lunch, Apoopan went and sat under a tree to rest. And soon enough Apoopan got a hiccup. He was very happy and said to himself

"Ha! Amooma must be thinking of me."

But then there was another hiccup, and then another, and then another, till Apoopan was tired of hiccups.

When Apoopan reached home that day he asked Amooma angrily

"Why couldn't you think of me a little less? Why did you have to go off limits about thinking of me?"

"Sorry. I am just a silly old woman" said Amooma hiding a smile.
